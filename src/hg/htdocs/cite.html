  Citing the UCSC Browser in a Journal Publication
  There are various ways to cite the UCSC Genome Browser for a journal publication. Most journals accept a cite to a stable web page (1). Be sure to include the assembly date relevant to your work -- neither the reviewer nor the reader can replicate your use of the genome browser or table browser without it.

Three articles (2-4) provide references for the Genome Browser, the BLAT alignment tool, and the Genome Browser Database respectively. Their PubMed abstracts will contain links to the site. The freeze date must still be provided. Third party tracks should be credited according to their source, which can be found on the track's description page or on the Genome Browser credits page.

Work pertaining to the human genome assembly should cite the Human Genome Consortium paper (5). When publishing results pertaining to human genome assemblies prior to the 22 Dec 2001 freeze, also cite the GigAssembler (6). Manuscripts that include the use of the mouse genome should cite the Mouse Genome Sequencing Consortium paper (7).

Journal quality screen shots of the Genome Browser can be produced by using the PDF/PS utility accessed via the top menu bar link on the tracks display page. When including a screen shot in your manuscript, reference http://genome.ucsc.edu in the caption and cite (2) and (4). Also mention the genome assembly used to produce the image.

For a extended list of publications produced by the UCSC Genome Bioinformatics group, see the Publications page.
