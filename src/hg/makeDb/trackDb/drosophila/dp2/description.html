The August 2003 Drosophila pseudoobscura genome assembly is based on the Freeze 1 assembly produced by the Human Genome Sequencing Center (HGSC) at Baylor School of Medicine.

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified by a scaffold coordinate range, the accession number of an mRNA or RefSeq entry, a gene name, or keywords from the GenBank description of an mRNA. The following list shows examples of valid position queries for the D. pseudoobscura genome. Note that some position queries (e.g. "huntington") may return matches to the mRNA records of other species. In these cases, the mRNAs are mapped to their homologs in D. pseudoobscura. See the User's Guide for more information.

Assembly details

Freeze 1 is a whole genome shotgun assembly produced using Baylor HGSC's assembly engine, Atlas. The assembly, which provides approximately 7x coverage of the euchromatic portion of the genome, contains 759 scaffolds. The scaffold N50 size is 1,018,646 bp. The total scaffold size for this assembly is 139.3 Mbp, with an average size of 184,465 bp. Due to an assembly error, four large scaffolds "jumped" chromosomes. These have been split into "A" and "B" parts in the downloadable assembly files:
  • Contig3286_Contig7811 was split into Contig3286_Contig7811A and Contig3286_Contig7811B.
  • Contig4971_Contig7717 was split into Contig4971_Contig7717A and Contig4971_Contig7717B.
  • Contig6811_Contig7852 was split into Contig6811_Contig7852A and Contig6811_Contig7852B.
  • Contig7891_Contig7492 was split into Contig7891_Contig7492A and Contig7891_Contig7492B.
Baylor HGSC has provided a putative chromosome assignment for the majority of larger scaffolds (> 90% of unique sequence), based on conservation between the Muller elements.

Bulk downloads of the sequence and annotation data are available via the Genome Browser FTP server or the Downloads page. These data are made available with specific conditions for use. The dp2 annotation tracks were generated by UCSC and collaborators worldwide.
