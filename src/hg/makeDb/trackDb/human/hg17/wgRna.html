Description

This track displays positions of three different types of RNA in the human genome: microRNAs from the miRNA Registry at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute and C/D box and H/ACA small nucleolar RNAs (snoRNAs) from the snoRNA-LBME-DB maintained at the Laboratoire de Biologie Moléculaire Eucaryote. C/D box and H/ACA box snoRNAs are guides for the 2'O-ribose methylation and the pseudouridilation, respectively, of rRNAs and snRNAs, although many of them have no documented target RNA.

Mature miRNAs (miRs) are represented by thick red blocks; the predicted stem-loop portions of the primary transcripts are indicated by thinner blocks. When a single precursor produces two mature miRs from its 5' and 3' parts, both positions of the mature miRNA are individually displayed.

C/D box and H/ACA box snoRNAs are represented by blue and green blocks, respectively. At a zoomed-in resolution, arrows superimposed on the blocks indicate the the sense orientation of the snoRNAs.

Methods

Mature and precursor miRNAs from the miRNA Registry were aligned against the genome using blat. The extents of the precursor sequences were not generally known and were predicted based on base-paired hairpin structure. The miRNA Registry is described in Griffiths-Jones, S., The microRNA Registry, Nucl. Acids Res. 32, D109-D111 (2004).

The snoRNAs from the snoRNA-LBME-DB were aligned against the human genome using blat.
