Description

Like DNA, mature RNA molecules carry chemical modifications that influence their fate. The most abundant internal modification of mRNA is N&sup6;-methyladenosine (m6A), deposited by the METTL3/METTL14 writer complex onto adenosines that lie in a specific 5'-DRACH-3' sequence context (D = A/G/U, R = A/G, H = A/C/U). m6A and related modifications regulate pre-mRNA processing, nuclear export, mRNA decay, and translation, and are increasingly implicated in development and disease.

This collection groups together tracks related to RNA chemical modifications. The current tracks are:

  • DRACH motif sites — every occurrence of the m6A consensus motif (DRACH) in MANE Select transcripts, projected onto the genome.
  • Fetal M6A from Xiao 2019 — experimentally measured m6A peaks in fetal human tissues from Xiao et al. 2019.
  • m6A-Atlas v2 sites — high-confidence base-resolution m6A sites compiled from 12 high-resolution detection technologies across 24 human cell lines and tissues, from m6A-Atlas v2.0 (Liang et al. 2024).
