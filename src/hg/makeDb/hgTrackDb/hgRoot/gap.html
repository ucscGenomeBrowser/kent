Description

This track depicts gaps in the assembly. These gaps - with the exception of intractable heterochromatic gaps - will be closed during the finishing process.

Gaps are represented as black boxes in this track. If the relative order and orientation of the contigs on either side of the gap is known, it is a 'bridged' gap and a white line is drawn through the black box representing the gap. There are four principle types of gaps:

  • Fragment - gaps between the contigs of a draft clone. These are represented by 100 N's in the assembly, whether or not there is bridging information.
  • Clone - gaps between clones in the same map contig. These are represented by 50,000 N's in the assembly if the gap is bridged or 100,000 N's if it is not.
  • Contig - gaps between map contigs. These are represented by 200,000 N's in the assembly.
  • Heterochromatin - gaps from centromeres (3,000,000 N's) or other large blocks of heterochromatin (size varies).
