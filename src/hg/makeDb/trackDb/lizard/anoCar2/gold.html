Description

This track shows the draft assembly (May 2010, Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard AnoCar 2.0 (GCA_000090745.1)) of the $organism genome. (WGS assembly information) Whole-genome shotgun reads were assembled into contigs. When possible, contigs were grouped into scaffolds (also known as "supercontigs"). The order, orientation and gap sizes between contigs within a scaffold are based on paired-end read evidence. Scaffolds have been assembled into chromosome linkage groups.

In dense mode, this track depicts the contigs that make up the currently viewed scaffold/chromosome. Contig boundaries are distinguished by the use of alternating gold and brown coloration. Where gaps exist between contigs, spaces are shown between the gold and brown blocks. The relative order and orientation of the contigs within a scaffold is always known; therefore, a line is drawn in the graphical display to bridge the blocks.

All components within this track are of fragment type "W": Whole Genome Shotgun contig.

The mitochondrion sequence NC_010972.2 is included as a "F" finished sequence.
